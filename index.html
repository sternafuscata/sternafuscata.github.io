William Yancey Brown

Biographical Information and Tools

Bill Brown is a nonresident Senior Fellow at the Atlantic Council. He retired from the federal government in June 2024 as the Chief Environmental Officer of the Bureau of Ocean Energy Management (BOEM), a position he had served in since November 2013. His previous positions include Science Advisor to Interior Secretary Bruce Babbitt and President & CEO of the Bishop Museum in Hawaii, the Academy of Natural Sciences in Philadelphia, and the Woods Hole Research Center in Massachusetts. Bill has chaired the boards of the Ocean Conservancy and the Global Heritage Fund, and been President of the Natural Science Collections Alliance. He has degrees from the University of Virginia (BA Biology), Johns Hopkins University (MAT), University of Hawaii (PhD Zoology), where he lived on an uninhabited island to study seabirds, and Harvard Law School (JD). He has authored many non-fiction publications and two novels: Valley of the Scorpion and Ruffner's Cave. He is married to Mary McLeod and they have two daughters, Julia and Emma.
